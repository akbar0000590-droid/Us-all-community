Nondiscrimination Policy

Last Updated: March 15, 2025

Equal Opportunity for All

The US-All Community Trust Relief Program prohibits discrimination in all its programs and activities.

Our Commitment

The US-All Community Trust Relief Program provides equal opportunity to all individuals and does not discriminate on the basis of:

  • Race, Color, or National Origin
  • Religion or Creed
  • Sex, Gender Identity, or Sexual Orientation
  • Age
  • Disability
  • Veteran or Military Status
  • Genetic Information
  • Citizenship or Immigration Status
  • Marital or Familial Status
  • Receipt of Public Assistance
  • Any other characteristic protected by federal, state, or local law

Program Access

We ensure equal access to all program services, including:

  • Application processing and consideration
  • Grant awards and disbursements
  • Technical assistance and support
  • Information and referrals
  • Participation in program activities

Language Access

We provide free language assistance services, including:

  • Interpreters for over 20 languages (available by phone or in-person)
  • Translated application materials in Spanish, Chinese, Vietnamese, Arabic, and Tagalog
  • Language identification cards to help staff identify your language needs
  • Bilingual staff at all office locations

To request language assistance, text 📞 (385) 281‑3672 and say your language.

Reasonable Accommodations

We provide reasonable accommodations for individuals with disabilities, including:

  • Alternative formats of documents (large print, braille, audio)
  • Sign language interpreters
  • Assistive technology compatibility
  • Accessible office locations
  • Flexible application procedures

To request an accommodation, contact our Accessibility Coordinator at usallcommunitytrustreliefprogr@gmail.com or 📞 (385) 281‑3672.

📢 Filing a Discrimination Complaint

If you believe you have been subjected to discrimination, you have the right to file a complaint.

How to File:

  1. Submit a written complaint to our Civil Rights Coordinator
  2. Include your name, contact information, and description of the alleged discrimination
  3. Provide details: dates, individuals involved, and any witnesses
  4. Submit within 180 days of the alleged incident

Where to Submit:

  • Email: usallcommunitytrustreliefprogr@gmail.com/li>
  • Phone:📞 (385) 281‑3672
  • Address: 🏛️ 2020 W El Camino Ave,
    Sacramento, CA United States 95833

Complaints will be investigated promptly and impartially. You will receive a written decision within 30 days.

Retaliation Prohibited

The US-All Community Trust Relief Program strictly prohibits retaliation against any individual who:

  • Files a discrimination complaint
  • Participates in an investigation
  • Opposes a discriminatory practice
  • Requests an accommodation or language assistance

If you believe you have experienced retaliation, please report it immediately.

Federal Coordination

As a recipient of federal funds, we comply with:

  • Title VI of the Civil Rights Act of 1964
  • Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973
  • Title II of the Americans with Disabilities Act
  • Age Discrimination Act of 1975
  • Title IX of the Education Amendments of 1972
